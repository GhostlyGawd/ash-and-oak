# Product direction

## Vision

Create a persistent fantasy world that exists independently of players, develops a believable history, and lets players live a genuine second life inside a detailed simulation with lasting consequences.

Ash & Oak is not “RuneScape with AI.” It is a living-world simulator inspired by RuneScape's freedom and skilling, Kenshi's physical consequences, Elite Dangerous's regional scale and economy, and Valheim's readable survival and settlement life.

AI is a supporting mechanism for continuity, interpretation, rumors, readable history, and plausible high-level decisions. AI is not the product and must not substitute for game systems.

## Emotional target

When returning, the player should ask:

> What happened while I was gone?

They should recognize the world while observing that life continued: people aged, businesses changed, roads became safer or more dangerous, property accumulated history, resources shifted, conflicts ended, and relationships evolved.

The player is returning to history, not consuming a content patch.

## Core loop

**Life → pressure → decision → consequence → adaptation → history**

There is no universal combat-first loop. A healer, farmer, merchant, explorer, innkeeper, criminal, soldier, and builder should experience the same world through different verbs and responsibilities.

## Pillars

### Persistent living world

Civilization, nature, settlements, factions, trade routes, property, disease, infrastructure, families, and careers continue over time. Important events accumulate instead of resetting.

### Sandbox second life

The player chooses a life rather than a class. Careers can change, but relationships, obligations, injuries, skills, legal history, property, and reputation preserve the past.

### Detailed simulation, light friction

The simulation should be deep under the surface without becoming meter babysitting or a spreadsheet. A need matters when it creates a decision: food matters to a siege, clinic, caravan, famine, or expedition—not because a hunger bar demands attention every few minutes.

### Body history and legacy

Injuries, scars, illness, age, apprentices, property, family, and reputation become a record of the life lived. Consequences should usually create adaptation and recovery rather than arbitrary deletion.

### Local agency, global consequence

Players primarily affect homes, workshops, farms, roads, bridges, inns, guild halls, docks, and settlements. Connected systems allow those local actions to become regional history.

## Interaction principles

1. The player should inhabit a body in a place.
2. World state should be observable before it is summarized.
3. Repeated ordinary routines make unusual change meaningful.
4. Important people act independently and can surprise the player.
5. Information is imperfect; decisions should not expose a complete optimization answer.
6. A verb must support learning and mastery.
7. The world can threaten attachment, but it should prefer damage, occupation, disruption, and recovery over unexplained deletion.
8. The environment is the primary output. Text and AI-generated history are secondary.

## Initial playable life

The first deep lifestyle is a healer in Riverward. It connects:

- Named patients and families
- Diagnosis and treatment
- Ingredient gathering and medicine preparation
- Clinic inventory and finances
- Guild, temple, legal, and underworld relationships
- Disease propagation and public health
- Weather, water, farms, markets, and travel
- Reputation, debt, injury, and professional mastery

The healer is a proving ground for the shared simulation architecture, not the final scope of the game.

## World fidelity

- **High fidelity:** player, nearby important NPCs, active property, current settlement, active conflicts and events.
- **Medium fidelity:** villages, farms, caravans, wildlife groups, military units, and regional economies.
- **Low fidelity:** distant individuals and background activity represented statistically until relevance requires promotion.

The simulation should zoom fidelity in and out without losing identity or causal history.

## Non-goals

Ash & Oak is not a chatbot village, a theme-park MMO, a quest treadmill, a chosen-one story, an infinite-content generator, a private base builder disconnected from the shared world, or a static dashboard covered in fantasy prose.
